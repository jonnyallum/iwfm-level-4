> Closed book. 90 minutes. Answer all three sections in your own words. Mark with the [marking guide](../17-marking-guides/marking-guide.md). Total 100 marks.

## Section A — Knowledge (30 marks, 5 each)

**A1.** State the core purpose of facilities management in one sentence.

**A2.** Give the five steps of risk assessment.

**A3.** Who is the "responsible person" under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, and name two of their duties.

**A4.** What conditions allow Legionella to grow, and what are the two temperature principles that control it?

**A5.** Explain the difference between opex and capex with one FM example of each.

**A6.** Name three FM KPIs and what each tells you.

## Section B — Application (30 marks, 10 each)

**B1.** A fire door on a public escape route is repeatedly wedged open for trolley access. Explain the risk and how you would resolve it permanently.

**B2.** You inherit an estate with no reliable asset register. Describe your first three moves and why.

**B3.** A contractor arrives to repair a roof leak above a public route. Their RAMS are generic. Describe how you control the work before it starts.

## Section C — Scenario (40 marks)

**C1.** It is peak season at a visitor attraction. On the same morning:

- a chiller serving the main restaurant fails;
- a water temperature monitoring record is found to have two months of gaps;
- a contractor wants the fire alarm isolated for two hours to fix a detector.

You cannot personally do everything at once. Explain how you triage these three issues, what you do about each, who you communicate with, and how you protect both safety and the day's trading. Justify your priority order.

## Marking Notes

- Section C carries the most marks and is where judgement is tested. A pass must prioritise life safety (alarm isolation control and the temperature-gap risk) while keeping the revenue area open through contingency, and must communicate clearly.
- Apply the automatic caps in the marking guide: any answer that trades safety for trading, or isolates the alarm with no fire-watch cover, is capped at Borderline.
- Award across knowledge, application, judgement and communication strands.

## Model Answer Pointers

- **A1-A6:** see the corresponding workbook files for model points (Modules 1, 2, 4, 5, 9, 11).
- **B1:** compartmentation/escape risk; remove wedge; fit alarm-linked hold-open device; address behaviour; record.
- **B2:** survey critical assets; capture condition and statutory status; stabilise compliance and identify highest-consequence items. Visibility before optimisation.
- **B3:** stop work starting; require site-specific RAMS (visitor segregation, fragile roof, rescue); check competence; agree access controls; issue any permit; brief operations; monitor and close out.
- **C1:** Triage by risk. Fire alarm isolation: only proceed with permit, minimised scope/time and fire-watch cover, areas informed. Water gaps: treat as a compliance/health risk — reinstate monitoring, increase flushing, assess the gap, consider sampling, record. Chiller: keep the restaurant open via temporary cooling/contingency and urgent attendance, assess for whole-life replacement given criticality. Communicate with operations, the contractor, and management; keep a decision log. Priority: life-safety controls first, then continuity of trading.
