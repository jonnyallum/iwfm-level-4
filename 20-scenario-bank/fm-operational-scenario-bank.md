## Plain English Summary

This bank contains 50 Facilities Management operational scenarios for Level 4-style learning, Longleat interview preparation and practical FM Control Hub training.

Each scenario asks the learner to identify risk, immediate controls, evidence, owner, escalation and longer-term prevention.

## Scenario Response Structure

Use this structure for each answer:

1. Make the situation safe.
2. Identify who is affected.
3. Check legal or compliance duties.
4. Involve competent people where needed.
5. Record evidence.
6. Assign actions and owners.
7. Escalate if risk, budget or authority requires it.
8. Review prevention.

## 50 Operational Scenarios

### 1. Fire Alarm Fault During Opening

Prompt: The fire alarm panel shows a fault while visitors are onsite.

Expected response: confirm impairment, call contractor, notify Responsible Person, apply temporary measures, record times and decisions, decide whether areas can remain open.

### 2. Emergency Lighting Failure

Prompt: A monthly test finds failed emergency lights on an escape route.

Expected response: assess escape route risk, raise urgent remedials, consider temporary lighting or route restriction, record defects and close with evidence.

### 3. Fire Door Not Closing

Prompt: A fire door to a plant room will not close.

Expected response: treat as life-safety defect, stop wedging/manual reliance, arrange repair, record temporary controls and review nearby doors.

### 4. Blocked Final Exit

Prompt: A delivery blocks a final exit during a busy day.

Expected response: clear immediately, speak to delivery owner, record non-conformance, review delivery procedure and monitor recurrence.

### 5. Hot Works Request in Heritage Area

Prompt: A contractor requests welding work in a heritage building.

Expected response: review RAMS, issue hot works permit, remove combustibles, manage detection isolation, fire watch, post-work checks and handback.

### 6. Missing Fire Risk Assessment Actions

Prompt: The FRA action tracker has not been updated for three months.

Expected response: reconcile actions, risk-rate overdue items, assign owners, escalate high-risk actions and rebuild reporting rhythm.

### 7. Out-of-Range Water Temperatures

Prompt: Several cold outlets are above control limits.

Expected response: escalate to Responsible Person, retest, restrict use if needed, seek contractor advice, track remedials and review risk assessment.

### 8. Missed Flushing Records

Prompt: A closed building wing has no flushing records for a month.

Expected response: identify affected outlets, flush or control before use, document gap, investigate cause and update regime.

### 9. Water Contractor Report Unread

Prompt: A water hygiene report contains recommendations but no one has actioned them.

Expected response: log each recommendation, risk-rate, assign owners, challenge unclear advice and report overdue items.

### 10. Occupancy Change Creates Low Use

Prompt: Offices become rarely used after a working pattern change.

Expected response: review water risk, update outlet register, amend flushing, consider removing redundant outlets and brief Responsible Person.

### 11. Suspected Asbestos Found

Prompt: A contractor uncovers suspicious board during drilling.

Expected response: stop work, isolate area, check register, obtain competent advice, record possible exposure and review contractor controls.

### 12. Asbestos Register Unavailable

Prompt: Urgent intrusive work is requested but the register cannot be accessed.

Expected response: do not proceed, find evidence, arrange survey/advice if needed, communicate delay and record decision.

### 13. Damaged Asbestos-Containing Material

Prompt: Known asbestos insulation board is damaged in a service riser.

Expected response: restrict access, contact competent asbestos contractor, update management plan, record remedials and inform affected parties.

### 14. EICR Unsatisfactory

Prompt: The EICR comes back unsatisfactory with C2 observations.

Expected response: review risk, instruct remedials, track certificates, consider temporary controls and report status.

### 15. Repeated Breaker Trips

Prompt: A distribution board trips repeatedly but staff keep resetting it.

Expected response: stop normalising reset, restrict use if needed, call electrician, investigate load/fault and record action.

### 16. Temporary Event Power

Prompt: An event team wants temporary power across a public route.

Expected response: require competent installation, cable protection, RCDs, weather controls, public segregation and pre-use sign-off.

### 17. Electrical Cupboard Storage

Prompt: Cleaning stock is stored in an electrical cupboard.

Expected response: remove stock, inspect, brief team, provide alternative storage and audit recurrence.

### 18. Lift Entrapment

Prompt: Visitors are trapped in a lift for 20 minutes.

Expected response: activate rescue process, communicate with passengers, call contractor, record times, review alarm and fault history.

### 19. LOLER Serious Defect

Prompt: A thorough examination identifies a serious lifting defect.

Expected response: remove from use where required, notify users, instruct remedial work, retain report and closure evidence.

### 20. PUWER Guard Removed

Prompt: A workshop machine is found with a guard removed.

Expected response: take out of use, investigate, retrain, repair guard, review supervision and record action.

### 21. Ladder Defect

Prompt: A ladder has damaged feet but is still in the store.

Expected response: quarantine, label, update register, replace or repair and remind users of pre-use checks.

### 22. Roof Access in High Winds

Prompt: A contractor wants to continue roof work despite high winds.

Expected response: stop or suspend work, review RAMS/weather limits, record decision and reschedule safely.

### 23. MEWP Near Visitor Queue

Prompt: A MEWP is needed near an active visitor queue.

Expected response: create exclusion zone, reroute visitors, check operator competence, rescue plan and spotter arrangements.

### 24. Scaffold Without Handover

Prompt: Scaffold is erected but no handover certificate is available.

Expected response: prevent use, request certificate/inspection, record issue and monitor contractor performance.

### 25. Contractor Without RAMS

Prompt: A contractor arrives for reactive repair without RAMS.

Expected response: do not start higher-risk work, obtain/review RAMS, use dynamic controls only if genuinely low risk and documented.

### 26. Generic RAMS

Prompt: RAMS do not mention the public area where work will happen.

Expected response: reject or request revision, include visitor segregation, emergency access, timing and monitoring.

### 27. Permit Not Closed

Prompt: A hot works permit remains open after the contractor leaves.

Expected response: inspect area, complete fire watch if required, close permit properly and review contractor behaviour.

### 28. Contractor Changes Method

Prompt: A contractor switches from tower scaffold to ladder.

Expected response: stop, review risk, update RAMS/permit, confirm suitability and record approval or rejection.

### 29. Chemical Decanted Into Unlabelled Bottle

Prompt: Cleaning chemical is found in an unlabelled spray bottle.

Expected response: remove from use, identify substance, relabel or dispose, retrain and review COSHH controls.

### 30. COSHH Spill

Prompt: A chemical spill occurs in a public toilet area.

Expected response: isolate area, use spill response, check exposure, dispose safely, record incident and review storage/use.

### 31. Waste Carrier Licence Missing

Prompt: A new waste contractor cannot provide carrier registration.

Expected response: do not release waste, verify registration, use approved carrier and record duty of care check.

### 32. Waste Transfer Note Missing

Prompt: Waste collections occurred but transfer notes are missing.

Expected response: request copies, check contractor process, record gap, prevent recurrence and escalate if repeated.

### 33. Pest Activity in Food Area

Prompt: Pest activity is reported near a visitor cafe.

Expected response: inform pest contractor, protect food area, inspect hygiene/building defects, log actions and verify close-out.

### 34. Visitor Path Trip Hazard

Prompt: Uneven paving is found on a high-footfall route.

Expected response: barrier or divert, photograph, risk-rate, repair, inspect similar areas and track completion.

### 35. Tree Limb Over Public Route

Prompt: A cracked tree limb is visible above a visitor path.

Expected response: close/divert route, call arborist, record photos, complete works and review tree inspection regime.

### 36. Animal Enclosure Gate Defect

Prompt: A primary enclosure gate latch is unreliable.

Expected response: notify animal team, restrict use, temporary control, competent repair, senior escalation and evidence.

### 37. Estate Road Flooding

Prompt: Heavy rain floods a service road used by visitors and staff.

Expected response: assess route safety, close/divert, check drainage, communicate, record and plan permanent improvement.

### 38. Public Toilet Outage

Prompt: A main toilet block fails during peak attendance.

Expected response: deploy alternative facilities, communicate, call contractor, manage hygiene, review resilience.

### 39. Power Outage

Prompt: A site-wide power outage occurs with 5,000 visitors onsite.

Expected response: incident command, life-safety systems, generators, visitor movement, animal-critical systems, board updates.

### 40. Generator Fails Test

Prompt: The emergency generator fails its monthly test.

Expected response: assess critical loads, call contractor, temporary contingency, escalate risk and track repair.

### 41. Maintenance Backlog Cut

Prompt: Budget cuts require backlog reduction.

Expected response: separate statutory/life-safety from discretionary, risk-rate, show options and formal decisions.

### 42. Critical Spare Unavailable

Prompt: A critical pump fails and replacement lead time is six weeks.

Expected response: temporary workaround, supplier escalation, risk assessment, communicate impact and review spares strategy.

### 43. Service Report With Hidden Defects

Prompt: A contractor report is filed but contains unresolved defects.

Expected response: extract defects, create actions, assign owners, escalate high risk and review evidence process.

### 44. Compliance Dashboard Shows False Green

Prompt: Dashboard says complete but high-risk actions are open.

Expected response: correct status logic, separate task completion from defects, report true risk and update schema.

### 45. Audit Finds Missing Evidence

Prompt: Audit cannot find records for several statutory tasks.

Expected response: identify missing periods, gather evidence, assess risk, create corrective plan and report gaps honestly.

### 46. Contractor Portal Access Ends

Prompt: A contractor contract ends and their portal contains service records.

Expected response: export evidence, store centrally, verify completeness and add offboarding step.

### 47. Board Asks If Site Is Safe

Prompt: The board asks whether the site can stay open after multiple issues.

Expected response: explain controls, impaired systems, compensatory measures, closure thresholds and decisions required.

### 48. Complaint About Unsafe Queue Area

Prompt: Visitors complain that a queue area feels overcrowded and poorly lit.

Expected response: inspect, manage crowding, improve lighting/signage, coordinate operations and record actions.

### 49. Emergency Services Access Concern

Prompt: A temporary event layout narrows emergency access.

Expected response: stop layout approval, revise plan, consult responsible teams, document emergency access route.

### 50. New FM Handover

Prompt: You inherit a site with weak records and many unknown assets.

Expected response: prioritise critical compliance, rebuild asset register, gather evidence, review contractors and brief leaders.

## Marking Guide

Strong scenario answers:

- act immediately where safety is at risk;
- name the competent person or team needed;
- link actions to legal or operational duties;
- record evidence and decisions;
- assign owners and dates;
- escalate blocked or high-risk items;
- include Longleat visitor and animal context where relevant.

Weak scenario answers:

- delay urgent controls;
- rely only on email;
- ignore public interfaces;
- fail to document decisions;
- treat contractor reports as automatically compliant;
- miss review and prevention.
