## What This Course Is

This is a practical Level 4-style Facilities Management course designed for mobile study, interview preparation and real operational use.

It is not an official IWFM qualification. It is a structured learning programme that helps you think and answer like an operational Facilities Manager.

## Who It Is For

This course is for learners who want to move beyond basic maintenance coordination and into proper Facilities Management thinking.

It is especially useful for people preparing for roles involving:

- estates management;
- compliance;
- contractors;
- public buildings;
- care settings;
- visitor attractions;
- high-footfall environments;
- health and safety responsibility;
- planned maintenance;
- asset management.

## How To Use It On A Phone

Use the course in short sessions.

A good rhythm is:

1. Read one phone lesson.
2. Write short notes in your own words.
3. Answer the scenario question.
4. Compare your answer to the model answer.
5. Rewrite your answer at manager level.

The aim is not to memorise paragraphs. The aim is to build judgement.

## The Level 4 Mindset

Level 4-style FM is not about knowing every technical detail yourself.

It is about being able to:

- identify risk;
- set priorities;
- manage contractors;
- protect people;
- maintain compliance evidence;
- control budgets;
- communicate clearly;
- make decisions under pressure;
- improve systems rather than repeatedly fighting fires.

## The Core FM Question

In almost every scenario, ask:

> What is the risk, who could be harmed, what controls are needed, what evidence proves it, and who owns the next action?

That question is the backbone of the course.

## Study Method

For each module, work through:

- learning outcomes;
- lessons;
- practical application;
- scenario questions;
- hard questions;
- model answers;
- marking guide;
- interview talking points.

## How To Answer Questions

Avoid weak answers like:

> I would get it fixed.

Use manager-level answers like:

> I would first make the area safe, assess the risk to staff and visitors, check whether the defect affects statutory compliance, assign ownership, record the evidence, set a timescale based on risk and review whether existing controls failed.

## Marking Standard

Answers are marked against:

- risk awareness;
- legal/compliance awareness;
- operational practicality;
- evidence trail;
- prioritisation;
- communication;
- prevention of recurrence.

## Link To Compliance Bible

This course should be studied alongside the Compliance Bible repository. The Compliance Bible explains the legal and compliance spine. This course teaches how to apply it as a Facilities Manager.

## First Assignment

Write a short answer to this question:

> What is the difference between fixing maintenance problems and managing Facilities Management risk?

A strong answer should include people, premises, processes, evidence, cost, compliance and prevention.
