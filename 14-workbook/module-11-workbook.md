## Knowledge

**Q1.** Explain the difference between operational, tactical and strategic FM with a time horizon for each.

**Q2.** Name four FM KPIs and what each one tells you.

## Application

**Q3.** The business wants to grow visitor numbers by 20%. What does that mean for FM and the estate?

**Q4.** How does a rising reactive-to-planned ratio and a growing backlog inform your planning?

## Judgement

**Q5.** How would you move an estate from reactive firefighting to planned, strategic FM?

**Q6.** Should grounds maintenance be kept in-house or outsourced? How do you decide?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** Operational = today (the leak, the call-out, safe running this shift). Tactical = this year (planned maintenance, budget, contracts, audits). Strategic = three to five years (where the estate is going and what investment that needs).
- **Q2.** Statutory compliance % (must be 100%); planned-vs-reactive ratio (more planned is healthier); asset condition/remaining life; SLA response/rectification times; energy cost trend; deferred backlog and its risk.
- **Q3.** More load on every system: wear, waste, utilities, toilets, catering, parking, safety/emergency capacity. Assess what fails first, identify constraints, and build the investment and resilience to support growth safely.
- **Q4.** They show the estate drifting toward failure. Use the signal to plan, sequence and fund work before the crisis — not after something closes in peak season.
- **Q5.** Stabilise compliance; build visibility (asset register, condition, baseline ratio); shift spend into planned maintenance; clear backlog by risk; set KPIs to show the trend; move the culture from "fix what breaks" to "prevent what we can"; report progress so it is funded.
- **Q6.** Decide on cost, control, quality, risk and flexibility. In-house gives control and site knowledge; outsourcing gives specialist capacity and scale. Compare whole-life cost and criticality; safety/brand-critical work warrants tighter control.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Lifts out of daily jobs; aligns FM to business outcomes; prioritises by risk and data; plans multi-year; argues investment in business language. |
| Pass | Knows the three levels and KPIs; sensible planning and sourcing logic. |
| Borderline | Stuck mostly in operations; weak multi-year thinking. |
| Not yet | Sees FM as cost centre; prioritises by age or noise. |
