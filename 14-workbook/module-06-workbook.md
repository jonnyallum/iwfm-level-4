## Knowledge

**Q1.** Name the main building services systems an FM is responsible for and what each does.

**Q2.** What is a BMS (building management system) and what does it give an FM?

## Application

**Q3.** Visitors complain a building is too hot in one area and too cold in another. How do you investigate before assuming the plant has failed?

**Q4.** Why does planned maintenance of HVAC matter for both comfort and compliance?

## Judgement

**Q5.** A chiller serving a busy restaurant is showing intermittent faults in a summer heatwave. What is your short-term and longer-term response?

**Q6.** The BMS shows everything is "normal" but staff report problems. How much do you trust the dashboard?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** HVAC (heating, ventilation, cooling), electrical/power, lighting, water/plumbing, drainage, fire systems, lifts, controls/BMS, and security systems — providing comfort, safety, power and a working environment.
- **Q2.** A central control and monitoring system for building services. It gives visibility, alarms, scheduling, energy data and the ability to diagnose and optimise rather than just react.
- **Q3.** Check zoning, controls, setpoints, occupancy and airflow before condemning plant. Comfort complaints are often control/balancing issues, not failures. Use data and walk the area.
- **Q4.** Planned maintenance keeps systems efficient and reliable (comfort, energy) and satisfies statutory duties such as ventilation hygiene and refrigerant/F-gas obligations. Run-to-failure risks closure and breach.
- **Q5.** Short term: increase monitoring, get it attended urgently, plan temporary cooling/contingency to keep the revenue area open, brief operations. Longer term: assess for replacement on whole-life cost given repeated failure and peak-season criticality.
- **Q6.** Trust but verify. A BMS reflects its sensors and setup, which can be wrong, miscalibrated or out of date. Believe credible human reports, investigate on site, and check sensor accuracy.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Diagnoses before condemning; links maintenance to compliance and revenue; treats BMS as a tool, not gospel; plans continuity for critical plant. |
| Pass | Knows the systems and BMS; investigates sensibly; maintains planned regimes. |
| Borderline | Lists systems but weak on diagnosis or compliance link. |
| Not yet | Reactive; assumes failure or blindly trusts dashboards. |
