## Knowledge

**Q1.** What is Legionella and why is it a facilities management concern?

**Q2.** What does ACOP L8 require duty holders to do?

## Application

**Q3.** What conditions allow Legionella to grow, and how does FM design controls to prevent them?

**Q4.** Why are infrequently used outlets ("dead legs" and low-use taps) a particular risk, and what is the control?

## Judgement

**Q5.** A water temperature monitoring record has gaps for the last two months and the responsible technician has left. What do you do?

**Q6.** A wing of the estate has been closed and is now reopening to visitors. What must happen to the water system before reopening?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** A bacterium that causes Legionnaires' disease, a potentially fatal pneumonia, when contaminated water droplets are inhaled. FM controls the water systems where it grows and spreads.
- **Q2.** Identify and assess the risk, manage it (appoint a responsible/competent person), prevent or control with a written scheme, monitor and keep records, and review.
- **Q3.** Stagnant water, temperatures roughly 20-45°C, nutrients/biofilm/scale, and droplet generation. Controls: keep hot water hot and cold water cold, keep water moving (flushing), remove dead legs, maintain temperatures and clean systems.
- **Q4.** Stagnant water sits at growth temperatures and seeds the system. Control by regular flushing, removing redundant pipework, and including low-use outlets in the monitoring regime.
- **Q5.** Treat it as a compliance gap and a risk. Reinstate monitoring immediately, appoint/assign a competent person, assess the period of missing data, increase checks/flushing, consider sampling, and record the gap and recovery actions.
- **Q6.** Before reopening: flush and disinfect as needed, confirm temperatures, sample/test if appropriate after stagnation, update the risk assessment and written scheme, and only reopen outlets once control is evidenced.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Understands growth conditions and designs proactive control; treats reopening and monitoring gaps as serious; never relies on luck. |
| Pass | Correct on Legionella, L8 and core controls; manages gaps and reopening sensibly. |
| Borderline | Knows the hazard but vague on the control regime. |
| Not yet | Underestimates the risk; no grasp of monitoring or written scheme. |
