## Knowledge

**Q1.** What does operational FM leadership look like in practice (name three consistent behaviours)?

**Q2.** Why does the same fact need telling three ways to a board, a peer and the workshop?

## Application

**Q3.** A technician keeps skipping the sign-off on completed planned maintenance, saying the paperwork is pointless. How do you handle the conversation?

**Q4.** The board challenges your spend in a meeting and you do not have the figure to hand. What do you say?

## Judgement

**Q5.** A contractor is rude to a junior member of your team in front of visitors. What is your immediate response and your follow-up?

**Q6.** How do you build trust with an in-house team that has seen managers come and go?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** Be clear about what good looks like; be visible especially when things go wrong; make the call, own it and explain it afterwards. Set the standard, remove blockers, back the team in public, correct in private.
- **Q2.** Audiences act on different things: board hears money/risk/reputation/continuity; peers hear impact/coordination; workshop hears task/safety/respect. Translate the facts without changing them.
- **Q3.** Acknowledge the skill and completed work, then be clear the record is the evidence that protects him and the team if something fails or is audited. Agree the standard, set a review date, follow up so it is seen to be real.
- **Q4.** Do not bluff. Give the honest position and commit to a precise figure by a stated time, then deliver it. Credibility comes from reliability, not pretending to know.
- **Q5.** Step in calmly, move it away from visitors, make clear the behaviour is unacceptable, and support the team member in the moment. Afterwards: record it and escalate to the contractor's employer if needed. Protect the team and visitor experience first.
- **Q6.** Consistency: be visible early, listen before changing things, deliver small promises reliably, back the team in public, be honest when you do not know. Do what you said you would, repeatedly.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Translates for every audience; handles conflict early and factually; never bluffs; protects team and visitor experience; earns trust through consistency. |
| Pass | Sound leadership behaviours; honest communication; deals with issues. |
| Borderline | Knows the ideas but avoids hard conversations or uses one register. |
| Not yet | Leads by authority only; bluffs or ducks conflict. |
