## Purpose

This workbook turns the course modules into active practice. Each module has a matching workbook file with questions, model answer points and marking criteria, so a learner can test understanding rather than only read.

The workbook is formative: it is for learning and self-assessment. The [mock exams](../16-mock-exams/) and [final assessment](../18-final-assessment/) are summative: they measure whether the learning has stuck.

## How to Use

1. Read the module first.
2. Answer the workbook questions in your own words, out loud or in writing, before looking at the model points.
3. Compare your answer to the model answer points and the [marking guide](../17-marking-guides/marking-guide.md).
4. Score yourself honestly with the band descriptors.
5. Note any weak tag (for example `water-hygiene` or `budgeting`) and revisit that module.

Answer in plain UK English. Aim for the language of an operating FM manager: decision, risk, evidence and outcome.

## Question Types

- **Knowledge** — recall a duty, regime or definition.
- **Application** — apply it to a realistic FM situation.
- **Judgement** — make and defend a decision under pressure or constraint.

Judgement questions carry the most marks. At Level 4, knowing the rule matters less than knowing what to do when rules, cost, safety and operations pull against each other.

## Scoring Bands

| Band | Score | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Distinction | 80-100% | Confident, evidence-led, decisions defended in business and risk terms. |
| Pass | 60-79% | Sound understanding, mostly applied, some gaps in judgement or evidence. |
| Borderline | 50-59% | Knowledge present but weak application; revisit the module. |
| Not yet | Below 50% | Re-read the module and re-attempt before moving on. |
