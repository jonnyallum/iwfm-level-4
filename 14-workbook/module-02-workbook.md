## Knowledge

**Q1.** Define hazard and risk, and explain the difference.

**Q2.** What are the five steps of risk assessment?

## Application

**Q3.** You assess a risk as low likelihood but catastrophic consequence (for example a major plant failure during peak season). How does that shape your control approach?

**Q4.** What is the hierarchy of control, and why is "tell people to be careful" near the bottom?

## Judgement

**Q5.** A risk assessment is two years old and the area has changed. A contractor is about to start work relying on it. What do you do?

**Q6.** The board wants to accept a risk to save money. How do you make sure that acceptance is informed and defensible?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** A hazard is something with the potential to cause harm; risk is the likelihood of that harm occurring combined with its severity. Hazard = potential; risk = likelihood × consequence.
- **Q2.** Identify hazards; decide who might be harmed and how; evaluate risks and decide controls; record findings; review and update.
- **Q3.** Low likelihood does not mean ignore. High consequence justifies resilience, redundancy, planned maintenance and a contingency plan even if the event is rare.
- **Q4.** Eliminate, substitute, engineering controls, administrative controls, PPE. Behaviour/PPE relies on people and fails more easily than designing the hazard out.
- **Q5.** Stop reliance on the old assessment; review and update it for the changed area before work starts. An out-of-date assessment is not a valid control.
- **Q6.** Present likelihood, consequence, who is exposed and the cost of control versus the cost of the event. Record that the board accepted a named, quantified residual risk — informed acceptance, documented.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Treats high-consequence/low-likelihood risk seriously; uses hierarchy of control correctly; makes risk acceptance explicit and documented. |
| Pass | Correct definitions and five steps; sensible controls; reviews dated assessments. |
| Borderline | Knows definitions but applies controls weakly or relies on PPE/behaviour first. |
| Not yet | Confuses hazard and risk; no structured assessment. |
