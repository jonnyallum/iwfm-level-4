## Knowledge

**Q1.** What does estate management cover beyond a single building?

**Q2.** Name four external-estate risks that are often underrated and the regime that controls each.

## Application

**Q3.** A tree near a public path looks unstable. What do you do now and what is your ongoing responsibility?

**Q4.** The boundary fence between your estate and a neighbour is failing and responsibility is unclear. How do you handle it?

## Judgement

**Q5.** A listed building needs essential safety work but conservation rules restrict what you can change. How do you proceed?

**Q6.** After heavy rain a car park floods, an access road breaks up, and a tree drops a limb on a footpath during open hours. How do you manage it?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** Land, grounds, roads, car parks, paths, drainage, boundaries, signage, utilities infrastructure and multiple buildings — the whole site as a connected system, where one external failure can stop the operation.
- **Q2.** Trees (tree safety survey regime); paths/roads (surface and lighting inspection); drainage (culvert/flood checks); boundaries (security/containment and recorded responsibility). Each defensible only with a regime and records.
- **Q3.** Make it safe now (cordon, restrict access) and get a competent arboriculturist to inspect and act. Ongoing: a proportionate tree safety regime — register, risk-based inspection frequency, recorded actions. The defence against a claim is a reasonable documented routine.
- **Q4.** Establish responsibility from deeds/lease/boundary agreement, not assumption. If yours, act; if shared/neighbour's, open a documented conversation. If it creates immediate risk (security, public, animal containment), make it safe now and resolve responsibility after.
- **Q5.** Safety is non-negotiable but the method is constrained. Engage conservation officers and heritage specialists early, choose the least-intrusive compliant solution, secure consent, use specialist contractors. If safety and conservation truly conflict, safety wins — documented.
- **Q6.** Manage as an estate-wide event: make the tree/footpath safe first, redirect visitors and traffic from the flooded car park and damaged road, investigate drainage/culverts for the cause, arrange tree and surface works, record decisions. Then fix the systemic regime so it is foreseen, not repeated.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Thinks estate-as-system; runs proportionate regimes for forgotten external risks; clear on boundary ownership; treats heritage as a constraint on method, never an excuse to skip safety. |
| Pass | Knows external risks and regimes; sensible handling of trees, boundaries and heritage. |
| Borderline | Focuses on buildings; treats external risk as low priority. |
| Not yet | No estate-wide thinking; lets heritage or uncertainty stall safety. |
