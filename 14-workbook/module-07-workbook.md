## Knowledge

**Q1.** What is an asset register and what core information should it hold?

**Q2.** What is the difference between planned, preventive, predictive and reactive maintenance?

## Application

**Q3.** How does asset condition and remaining life inform a capital replacement programme?

**Q4.** What is "maintenance backlog" and why is a growing backlog a warning sign?

## Judgement

**Q5.** You inherit an estate with no reliable asset register. What are your first three moves?

**Q6.** Two assets need replacing and you can only fund one. How do you decide which?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** A record of every significant asset: identity/location, type, age, condition, expected and remaining life, maintenance history, criticality and replacement cost. It is the basis for planning and prioritisation.
- **Q2.** Reactive: fix on failure. Planned/preventive: scheduled maintenance to prevent failure. Predictive: condition/data-based intervention before failure. Each shifts further from firefighting toward control.
- **Q3.** Condition and remaining life let you forecast when assets must be replaced, sequence capital by risk and timing, and avoid both premature spend and run-to-failure surprises.
- **Q4.** Deferred maintenance that has not been done. A growing backlog means the estate is drifting toward failures, hidden risk and a future cost spike — a signal to invest before something breaks in peak season.
- **Q5.** Survey and build a register of critical assets first; capture condition and statutory status; identify the highest-risk/highest-consequence items and stabilise compliance. Visibility before optimisation.
- **Q6.** Prioritise by risk and business impact, not age: consequence of failure, compliance status, resilience (is there backup?), remaining life and whole-life cost. Fund the one whose failure hurts most; document the deferred risk.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Uses condition/criticality data to drive decisions; prioritises by risk and whole-life cost; treats backlog as a strategic signal. |
| Pass | Knows register and maintenance types; sensible prioritisation. |
| Borderline | Understands assets but prioritises by age or noise. |
| Not yet | No structured asset thinking; reactive only. |
