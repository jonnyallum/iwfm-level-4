## Knowledge

**Q1.** What is the core purpose of facilities management in one sentence?

**Q2.** Name three things an FM is responsible for that are not "fixing buildings".

## Application

**Q3.** A department head treats you as "the person who unblocks toilets". How do you reframe the FM role in that conversation without being defensive?

**Q4.** Give one example each of hard FM and soft FM on a visitor attraction site.

## Judgement

**Q5.** Two requests land at once: a manager wants a meeting room rearranged for a VIP, and a cleaner reports a slippery floor in a public area. Which do you prioritise and why?

**Q6.** Explain how good FM protects revenue and reputation, not just the building, using a visitor-attraction example.

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** FM makes the built environment safe, compliant, efficient and fit for the organisation's core purpose, so the business can operate.
- **Q2.** Compliance and statutory duties; people's safety; budgets and value; contractor control; service quality and visitor experience; business continuity.
- **Q3.** Acknowledge the request, then widen it: FM keeps the site safe, legal and open, which is what lets every department do its job. Frame as enabler, not handyman, with a concrete example.
- **Q4.** Hard FM: mechanical/electrical, fire systems, fabric, lifts. Soft FM: cleaning, security, catering support, grounds, waste, helpdesk.
- **Q5.** Safety first: the slippery floor is an immediate public-harm and liability risk. Make it safe (cordon/sign) before the discretionary room change; delegate or sequence the VIP request.
- **Q6.** A failed asset or unsafe area can close a revenue space, trigger a claim, or become a reputation story. FM protects continuity, brand and income, so it is a business function, not an overhead.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | FM framed as a business enabler; safety prioritised instinctively; revenue/reputation/continuity language used naturally. |
| Pass | Correct duties and hard/soft split; safety prioritised; some business framing. |
| Borderline | Lists tasks but sees FM as reactive maintenance only. |
| Not yet | Treats FM as "fixing things"; no safety or business awareness. |
