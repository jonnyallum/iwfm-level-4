## Knowledge

**Q1.** Who is the "responsible person" under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 and what must they do?

**Q2.** What is the purpose of a fire risk assessment and how often should it be reviewed?

## Application

**Q3.** A fire door is wedged open on a public route "because it is easier for the trolleys". Explain the problem and your response.

**Q4.** What everyday FM failures most often defeat fire safety in practice?

## Judgement

**Q5.** The fire alarm panel shows a fault on one zone during opening hours in a busy building. What do you do?

**Q6.** A contractor needs to isolate the fire alarm for two hours to work on a detector. How do you control this safely?

## Model Answer Points

- **Q1.** The person with control of the premises (often employer/occupier). They must carry out and maintain a fire risk assessment, put in place and maintain fire precautions, provide information and training, and plan for emergencies.
- **Q2.** To identify fire hazards, people at risk and the precautions needed. Review regularly, after significant change, after a fire/near-miss, or if it is no longer valid — at least annually as good practice.
- **Q3.** A wedged fire door defeats compartmentation and lets fire and smoke spread along the escape route. Remove the wedge, explain the risk, find a compliant solution (hold-open device linked to the alarm), and address the behaviour.
- **Q4.** Blocked or locked escape routes; wedged fire doors; missed door/extinguisher/alarm checks; storage in protected routes; disabled detection; poor contractor hot-works control.
- **Q5.** Treat the zone as unprotected: increase monitoring/fire watch on that area, get it investigated and repaired urgently, consider whether continued occupation is safe, and record the actions. Do not simply silence and ignore it.
- **Q6.** Permit-controlled isolation: minimum scope and time, alternative cover (fire watch, evacuation brief), affected areas informed, isolation logged, and confirmed reinstatement and test before sign-off.

## Marking Criteria

| Band | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Distinction | Understands compartmentation and escape; controls alarm isolation by permit with cover; never tolerates wedged doors or blocked routes. |
| Pass | Correct duties and FRA; fixes common failures; manages faults sensibly. |
| Borderline | Knows the law but weak on practical control. |
| Not yet | Treats fire safety as a tick-box; tolerates unsafe shortcuts. |
