## Purpose

This is the intensive, standalone plan for someone with **seven days** until a Longleat-style operational FM interview. It is harder and more structured than the light route in [How to use this for Longleat interview prep](../HOW_TO_USE_FOR_LONGLEAT_INTERVIEW_PREP.md). It uses the whole academy — modules, [workbook](../14-workbook/README.md), [mock exams](../16-mock-exams/), [final assessment](../18-final-assessment/final-assessment.md), [interview bank](longleat-interview-question-bank.md), [scenario bank](../20-scenario-bank/fm-operational-scenario-bank.md) and [boardroom simulation](../13-longleat-simulation/longleat-boardroom-simulation.md).

## How to Use This Plan

- Each day has three blocks: a **morning learn** block, a **commute/phone drill** block, and an **evening practice** block. Skip blocks if you must, but never skip the spoken practice.
- Always answer **out loud**. Reading is not rehearsing. The interview is spoken, so practise spoken.
- Mark yourself honestly against the [marking guide](../17-marking-guides/marking-guide.md). Note every weak tag and revisit it.
- Build your answers on the [STAR answer bank](longleat-star-answer-bank.md): Situation, Task, Action, Result.
- The single thread running through every day: **safety first, decision made, evidence kept, business framed.**

## Daily Time Budget

| Block | Time | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Morning learn | 30-45 min | Read and understand two topics. |
| Commute / phone drill | 15-20 min | Recall and flashcard-style questions out loud. |
| Evening practice | 30-45 min | Spoken answers, marked against the guide. |

A committed learner needs roughly **75-110 minutes a day**. If you only have one block, make it the evening spoken practice.

## Day 1 — Foundations and Risk

**Morning learn:** [FM Principles](../01-fm-principles/module-01-fm-principles.md) and [Risk Management](../02-risk-management/module-02-risk-management.md). Fix two ideas in your mind: FM is a business enabler, not a repair desk; and risk = likelihood × consequence, controlled by the hierarchy of control.

**Commute drill:** Out loud, answer [Module 1 workbook](../14-workbook/module-01-workbook.md) and [Module 2 workbook](../14-workbook/module-02-workbook.md) knowledge questions.

**Evening practice:** Answer 8 questions from the [interview bank](longleat-interview-question-bank.md) on FM role and risk, spoken, in STAR form. Mark each one.

**End-of-day check:** Can you explain in one sentence why FM protects revenue and reputation, not just buildings?

---

## Day 2 — Safety, Fire and the Non-Negotiables

**Morning learn:** [Health & Safety Management](../03-health-safety/module-03-health-safety-management.md) and [Fire Safety](../04-fire-safety/module-04-fire-safety.md). Lock in: near-misses are free warnings; you never trade safety for trading; wedged fire doors and blocked escape routes are never acceptable.

**Commute drill:** [Module 3 workbook](../14-workbook/module-03-workbook.md) and [Module 4 workbook](../14-workbook/module-04-workbook.md) questions, spoken.

**Evening practice:** Answer the "manager wants to keep an unsafe area open" and "fire alarm fault during opening hours" scenarios out loud. Then mark against the [marking guide](../17-marking-guides/marking-guide.md) — check you prioritised life safety first.

**End-of-day check:** Could you stand firm, politely, against commercial pressure to keep an unsafe area open?

---

## Day 3 — Water Hygiene and Building Systems

**Morning learn:** [Water Hygiene](../05-water-hygiene/module-05-water-hygiene.md) and [Building Systems](../06-building-systems/module-06-building-systems.md). Lock in: Legionella growth conditions and the hot/cold temperature principles; and diagnose before you condemn plant.

**Commute drill:** [Module 5 workbook](../14-workbook/module-05-workbook.md) and [Module 6 workbook](../14-workbook/module-06-workbook.md) questions, spoken.

**Evening practice:** Answer the "reopening a closed wing's water system" and "chiller failing in a heatwave" scenarios out loud, in STAR form. Mark them.

**End-of-day check:** Can you explain what must happen to a water system before a closed area reopens to visitors?

---

## Day 4 — Assets, Contractors and Money

**Morning learn:** [Asset Management](../07-asset-management/module-07-asset-management.md), [Contractor Management](../08-contractor-management/module-08-contractor-management.md) and [Budgeting & Finance](../09-budgeting-finance/module-09-budgeting-finance.md). Lock in: prioritise by risk not age; treat contractors as part of the site risk profile; argue on whole-life cost and run a no-surprises forecast.

**Commute drill:** [Module 7](../14-workbook/module-07-workbook.md), [Module 8](../14-workbook/module-08-workbook.md) and [Module 9](../14-workbook/module-09-workbook.md) workbook questions, spoken.

**Evening practice:** Build, out loud, the capital business case to replace a repeatedly-failing AHU serving the restaurant. Then sit **[Mock Exam 1](../16-mock-exams/mock-exam-1.md)** Section C and mark it.

**End-of-day check:** Can you turn "this asset is old" into a board-ready argument about risk, revenue and whole-life cost?

---

## Day 5 — Leadership, Strategy, Estate and the Boardroom

**Morning learn:** [Leadership & Communication](../10-leadership-communication/module-10-leadership-communication.md), [Strategic FM](../11-strategic-fm/module-11-strategic-fm.md) and [Estate Management](../12-estate-management/module-12-estate-management.md). Lock in: translate the same fact for board, peers and workshop; operational vs tactical vs strategic; manage the external estate as a connected system.

**Commute drill:** [Module 10](../14-workbook/module-10-workbook.md), [Module 11](../14-workbook/module-11-workbook.md) and [Module 12](../14-workbook/module-12-workbook.md) workbook questions, spoken.

**Evening practice:** Work through the [Longleat Boardroom Simulation](../13-longleat-simulation/longleat-boardroom-simulation.md) out loud, taking the FM seat. Focus on board-level risk communication and not bluffing figures.

**End-of-day check:** Can you reframe FM as a business enabler to a sceptical board chair, in their language?

---

## Day 6 — Full Mock Under Pressure

**Morning learn:** Re-read your weak tags from Days 1-5 only. Do not start new material.

**Commute drill:** 20 rapid questions from the [interview bank](longleat-interview-question-bank.md), spoken, no notes.

**Evening practice:** Sit **[Mock Exam 2](../16-mock-exams/mock-exam-2.md)** fully and timed, then mark it against the guide. Identify your three weakest areas.

**End-of-day check:** Did you make clear decisions under time pressure, or did you hedge? Hedging loses marks and loses interviews.

---

## Day 7 — Polish, STAR Stories and Calm

**Morning learn:** Read the [STAR answer bank](longleat-star-answer-bank.md). Choose and rehearse **five** STAR stories you can adapt to almost any question (a safety call, a contractor challenge, a budget/whole-life decision, a leadership/conflict moment, an incident you managed).

**Commute drill:** Rehearse your 60-second answer to "tell me about yourself" and "why this role at Longleat", out loud.

**Evening practice:** Do a light run of the [final assessment](../18-final-assessment/final-assessment.md) Part 4 integrated scenario, spoken, focusing on triage and communication — not writing it all out. Then stop, and rest. Do not cram the night before.

**End-of-day check:** Can you tell five strong STAR stories cleanly, and triage a multi-event peak-season morning out loud?

---

## The Five Things to Walk In Believing

1. **Safety is never traded.** Life safety and statutory duties come before trading, cost or convenience.
2. **Decisions, not hedging.** Make the call and defend it. "It depends" without a decision loses.
3. **Evidence is the defence.** Records, review dates and follow-up protect you, the team and the organisation.
4. **Whole-life cost beats lowest price.** Argue value and risk, not just the invoice.
5. **No surprises.** Bad news goes up early with a plan attached.

## Night-Before Checklist

- [ ] Five STAR stories rehearsed out loud.
- [ ] 60-second "about me" and "why Longleat" ready.
- [ ] Can name the Longleat-specific themes (visitor safety, animal infrastructure, fire, water hygiene, asbestos, power outage, backlog, board communication).
- [ ] Route, time and questions-for-them prepared.
- [ ] Stopped cramming; rested.
