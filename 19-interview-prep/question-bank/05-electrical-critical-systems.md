> Questions 41-50 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

41. How would you manage an EICR with C2 observations?
   Strong answer should cover: understand risk, competent remedial work, evidence and close-out.

42. What would you do after repeated circuit breaker trips?
   Strong answer should cover: stop normalising failure, investigate, contractor advice and risk controls.

43. How do you manage temporary electrical supplies for events?
   Strong answer should cover: competent design, protection, inspection, weather, public segregation and sign-off.

44. What is the difference between PAT and electrical safety management?
   Strong answer should cover: portable equipment versus whole electrical system risk.

45. How would you respond to a power outage with 5,000 visitors onsite?
   Strong answer should cover: life safety, critical systems, visitor communication, generators and incident log.

46. How do you decide which building systems are critical?
   Strong answer should cover: life safety, legal duty, public impact, animal welfare and business continuity.

47. How would you manage lift reliability issues?
   Strong answer should cover: fault trend, contractor challenge, accessibility, LOLER and lifecycle planning.

48. What evidence should prove electrical compliance?
   Strong answer should cover: EICR, remedials, contractor competence, permits and temporary supply sign-off.

49. What would you do if electrical cupboards were used for storage?
   Strong answer should cover: clear immediately, explain risk, inspect, prevent recurrence and monitor.

50. How would you brief the board on generator resilience?
   Strong answer should cover: load served, test regime, fuel, maintenance, failure risk and investment need.
