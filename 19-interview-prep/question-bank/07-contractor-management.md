> Questions 61-70 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

61. What makes contractor management effective?
   Strong answer should cover: approval, scope, RAMS, induction, permits, monitoring and review.

62. How do you review RAMS?
   Strong answer should cover: task-specific content, site hazards, competence, emergency arrangements and interfaces.

63. What would you do if a contractor arrived without approved RAMS?
   Strong answer should cover: do not start, review documents, reschedule or control urgent work properly.

64. How do you manage contractors in public areas?
   Strong answer should cover: segregation, timing, signage, supervision and visitor communication.

65. What contractor documents should be checked before approval?
   Strong answer should cover: insurance, competence, training, accreditations, RAMS and licences where relevant.

66. How do you handle a familiar contractor who ignores site rules?
   Strong answer should cover: stop behaviour, record non-conformance, escalate and review approval.

67. When would you use a permit to work?
   Strong answer should cover: hot works, roof access, isolations, confined spaces, intrusive works and high risk.

68. What is good permit close-out?
   Strong answer should cover: inspection, handback, evidence, system reinstatement and closure signature.

69. How would you manage hot works in a heritage building?
   Strong answer should cover: permit, combustibles, detection, fire watch, contractor competence and post-check.

70. How do you measure contractor performance?
   Strong answer should cover: safety, quality, response, evidence, defects, communication and repeat issues.
