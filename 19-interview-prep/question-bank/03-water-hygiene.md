> Questions 21-30 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

21. What are the key parts of a Legionella control system?
   Strong answer should cover: risk assessment, written scheme, Responsible Person, monitoring and action.

22. What would you do if water hygiene records were missing?
   Strong answer should cover: scope of gap, current checks, competent advice, risk review and evidence rebuild.

23. How would you respond to out-of-range water temperatures?
   Strong answer should cover: escalation, retesting, temporary controls, contractor advice and remedial action.

24. How do occupancy changes affect water hygiene?
   Strong answer should cover: low-use outlets, flushing, risk assessment review and dead legs.

25. What is the difference between a water risk assessment and a logbook?
   Strong answer should cover: risk identification versus evidence of control.

26. How would you challenge a water hygiene contractor report?
   Strong answer should cover: unclear recommendations, repeated findings, risk basis and action tracking.

27. What would you do if flushing had been missed in a closed area?
   Strong answer should cover: restrict use if needed, flush, check risk, record gap and prevent recurrence.

28. How would you brief a duty manager on Legionella controls?
   Strong answer should cover: simple risk explanation, tasks, failures and escalation.

29. What water hygiene evidence would you expect in an audit?
   Strong answer should cover: risk assessment, scheme, logs, remedials, training and contractor reports.

30. How would you reduce water hygiene costs without weakening compliance?
   Strong answer should cover: scope review, asset accuracy, competent input and documented justification.
