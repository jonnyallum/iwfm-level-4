> Questions 81-90 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

81. What makes FM different in a visitor attraction?
   Strong answer should cover: public safety, peak footfall, animal welfare, events, heritage and reputation.

82. How would you manage FM during peak season?
   Strong answer should cover: planned works timing, reactive cover, inspections, contractor control and communications.

83. How do animal welfare interfaces affect FM decisions?
   Strong answer should cover: enclosure integrity, services, emergency access, specialist teams and escalation.

84. What would you do if an animal enclosure gate defect was reported?
   Strong answer should cover: notify animal team, restrict access, assess containment, repair and board escalation if critical.

85. How would you manage visitor route safety?
   Strong answer should cover: inspections, surface condition, barriers, lighting, weather and incident trends.

86. How do you coordinate FM with operations on a busy day?
   Strong answer should cover: duty briefing, incident channels, priorities, escalation and recovery.

87. What would you do if emergency access was blocked by deliveries?
   Strong answer should cover: clear route, control supplier behaviour, record, communicate and prevent recurrence.

88. How would you prepare FM for a large public event?
   Strong answer should cover: utilities, toilets, waste, fire routes, contractors, temporary structures and contingency.

89. How do you handle media-sensitive FM failures?
   Strong answer should cover: facts, incident log, communications team, actions and no speculation.

90. What would you include in a visitor attraction FM risk register?
   Strong answer should cover: public routes, fire, water, asbestos, electrical, animal infrastructure, events and contractors.
