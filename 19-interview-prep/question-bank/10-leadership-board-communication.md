> Questions 91-100 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

91. How would you structure a board update after a serious FM incident?
   Strong answer should cover: known facts, risk, controls, actions, decisions and next update.

92. How do you lead under pressure when several systems fail?
   Strong answer should cover: command structure, prioritisation, delegation, evidence and communication rhythm.

93. How do you admit compliance gaps without sounding out of control?
   Strong answer should cover: clarity, temporary controls, action plan, owners, dates and decisions needed.

94. What does good FM governance look like?
   Strong answer should cover: policies, owners, evidence, dashboards, audits, escalation and review.

95. How do you challenge senior leaders constructively?
   Strong answer should cover: risk language, options, consequences, evidence and recommendation.

96. How do you build trust with operational teams?
   Strong answer should cover: responsiveness, plain communication, consistency, safety and practical solutions.

97. How would you explain the value of FM Control Hub?
   Strong answer should cover: single source of truth, evidence, tasks, dashboards, escalation and audit readiness.

98. How would you use Compliance Hub in interview preparation?
   Strong answer should cover: legal grounding, evidence examples, scenario practice and consistent language.

99. What would your first 90 days at Longleat focus on?
   Strong answer should cover: critical compliance, asset data, contractors, backlog, emergency plans and relationships.

100. What is your strongest FM leadership principle?
   Strong answer should cover: practical risk ownership, evidence, calm escalation and service continuity.
