> Questions 31-40 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

31. What would you do if a contractor discovered suspected asbestos?
   Strong answer should cover: stop work, isolate, register check, competent advice and exposure record.

32. How do you prevent accidental asbestos disturbance?
   Strong answer should cover: register access, permits, contractor induction and refurbishment surveys.

33. What is the duty to manage asbestos?
   Strong answer should cover: find, assess, record, plan, inform and review.

34. How would you manage intrusive works in a heritage building?
   Strong answer should cover: survey scope, permissions, services, fabric protection and competent contractors.

35. What would make an asbestos register unreliable?
   Strong answer should cover: old survey, missing areas, refurbishment changes and inaccessible information.

36. How do you brief contractors about site hazards?
   Strong answer should cover: asbestos, fire routes, access, public areas, welfare and emergency arrangements.

37. What would you do if an old building had no asbestos information?
   Strong answer should cover: presume risk, stop intrusive work, survey and management plan.

38. How would you manage roof leaks affecting building fabric and compliance?
   Strong answer should cover: make safe, protect systems, investigate cause, repair priority and evidence.

39. How do you keep building fabric defects from becoming safety issues?
   Strong answer should cover: inspections, criticality, temporary controls and planned repair.

40. How would you explain fabric maintenance risk to finance?
   Strong answer should cover: lifecycle cost, safety, heritage protection and failure consequence.
