> Questions 1-10 of the [Longleat interview question bank](../longleat-interview-question-bank.md). Answer aloud, then refine into STAR. Mark with the [spoken-answer rubric](../../17-marking-guides/spoken-answer-rubric.md).

1. How do you use risk assessment as a management tool rather than a paperwork exercise?
   Strong answer should cover: hazard, affected people, controls, evidence, ownership and review.

2. Tell me about a time you had to stop work because it was unsafe.
   Strong answer should cover: authority, communication, alternative plan and documented decision.

3. How would you prioritise 30 open defects with limited budget?
   Strong answer should cover: life safety, statutory compliance, public impact, criticality and escalation.

4. What would make you escalate a defect to senior management?
   Strong answer should cover: intolerable risk, blocked funding, repeated failure and legal exposure.

5. How do you make sure temporary controls do not become permanent?
   Strong answer should cover: due dates, owners, review cadence and dashboard visibility.

6. How would you manage a slip risk on a busy visitor route during heavy rain?
   Strong answer should cover: immediate controls, route diversion, inspection frequency and permanent fix.

7. How do you handle a near miss that did not cause injury?
   Strong answer should cover: investigation, trend review, corrective action and learning.

8. What does good evidence look like in FM health and safety?
   Strong answer should cover: dated, site-specific, reviewed, linked to actions and retrievable.

9. How do you balance operational pressure with safety controls?
   Strong answer should cover: proportionate control, risk explanation and no unsafe shortcuts.

10. How would you brief a non-technical manager about a high-risk FM issue?
   Strong answer should cover: plain English, consequence, options, recommendation and decision needed.
