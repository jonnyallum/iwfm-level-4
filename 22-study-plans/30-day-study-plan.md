## Purpose

A steady, four-week route through the whole academy for someone who has about a month and wants to learn the material properly — not cram. It paces one topic at a time, builds recall with [flashcards](../21-flashcards/flashcard-bank.md), tests with the [workbook](../14-workbook/README.md), and finishes with the [mock exams](../16-mock-exams/) and [final assessment](../18-final-assessment/final-assessment.md).

If your interview is sooner, use the [7-day crash plan](../19-interview-prep/7-day-longleat-crash-plan.md) instead. Track your progress in the [progress tracker](progress-tracker.md).

## How to Use

- Roughly **30-40 minutes a day**, five to six days a week.
- Each day: read or revise, then drill that topic's flashcards out loud, then answer the matching workbook questions.
- Mark workbook answers against the [marking guide](../17-marking-guides/marking-guide.md) and log your band.
- Rest days are real — recall improves with spacing, not constant cramming.

| 30 | Light review and rest | Skim weak cards; stop cramming. You're ready. |

## Finishing Standard

By Day 30 you should be able to:

- explain every module's core duty and one practical application;
- answer any workbook question to at least a Pass band;
- score a Pass or better on both mock exams and the final assessment;
- tell five STAR stories cleanly and triage a multi-event scenario out loud;
- hold a safety-first decision under boardroom pressure.
