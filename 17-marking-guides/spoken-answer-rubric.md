## Purpose

A rubric for marking **spoken** interview answers, as distinct from written exam answers. Use it with the [role-play prompts](../19-interview-prep/longleat-boardroom-roleplay-prompts.md), the [crash plan](../19-interview-prep/7-day-longleat-crash-plan.md) and the [interview bank](../19-interview-prep/longleat-interview-question-bank.md).

A spoken answer is judged on the same FM judgement as a written one (see the [marking guide](marking-guide.md)), plus delivery: an interviewer hears confidence, structure and the ability to hold a decision under pressure, not just content.

## What an Interviewer Is Listening For

1. **A decision** — did you actually decide, or hedge?
2. **Safety first** — was life safety and compliance instinctive?
3. **Structure** — could they follow your answer (STAR)?
4. **Composure** — did you hold your judgement when pushed?
5. **Right language** — board, peer or workshop, as the question demands?
6. **Brevity** — did you make your point and stop, or ramble?

## Scoring Dimensions

Score each dimension 0-4. Total out of 24.

| Dimension | 0-1 (weak) | 2 (developing) | 3-4 (strong) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Decision** | No decision; "it depends" with no landing. | A decision, but vague or late. | Clear, early decision, defended. |
| **Safety priority** | Trades safety for cost/speed. | Mentions safety but not first. | Safety and compliance instinctive and first. |
| **Structure (STAR)** | Rambling, no shape. | Some structure, drifts. | Clean situation-task-action-result. |
| **Composure under pressure** | Caves or flusters when pushed. | Wobbles but recovers. | Holds the line calmly and respectfully. |
| **Audience language** | Wrong register; jargon at a board. | Mostly right register. | Pitches perfectly to the listener. |
| **Brevity and close** | Rambles, no end. | A bit long. | Makes the point and stops. |

## Bands

| Band | Score /24 | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Interview-ready | 19-24 | Confident, decisive, safe, well-structured. Would impress a panel. |
| Nearly there | 14-18 | Sound judgement; tighten structure, brevity or composure. |
| Developing | 10-13 | Right instincts but unclear delivery or hesitant decisions. |
| Not yet | Below 10 | Re-rehearse with the STAR bank before sitting a real panel. |

## Automatic Caps

Cap a spoken answer at **Developing**, regardless of polish, if it:

- caves on safety or a statutory duty when the interviewer pushes;
- bluffs a figure or fact instead of committing to verify;
- relies on contractor or staff reputation instead of evidence;
- never actually lands on a decision.

A smooth, confident answer that does one of these has failed the thing the panel is really testing.

## Delivery Tells to Coach

**Mark up:** makes a decision in the first two sentences; pauses instead of filling; says "I don't have that figure, I'll confirm by end of day" rather than bluffing; finishes cleanly.

**Mark down:** "um, it depends, I suppose"; talking past the point; agreeing with the interviewer's pressure to seem agreeable; technical detail dump at board level; trailing off without a result.

## Feedback Format

After each answer, give exactly three things:

1. **Band and score** (e.g. "Nearly there, 16/24").
2. **The strongest moment** ("you prioritised the alarm isolation cover immediately").
3. **The single most useful fix** ("decide faster — say the decision first, then justify it").

## Notes for an AI Tutor

- Score the six dimensions, sum to /24, state the band.
- Always credit a well-justified alternative decision; you are marking judgement and delivery, not a single "right" answer.
- Identify the weak dimension (for example `composure` or `brevity`) so practice can target it.
- If the candidate caved on safety under pressure, apply the cap and say so plainly — that is the most important coaching note you can give.
