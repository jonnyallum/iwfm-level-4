## Purpose

This is the single marking standard for the workbook, mock exams and final assessment. Use it to grade your own answers, to mark a learner's answers, or as the rubric an AI tutor applies. It is written so a human or an AI can apply it consistently.

## Core Principle

At Level 4, marks reward **judgement**, not recall. A learner who can quote a regulation but cannot decide what to do when safety, cost and operations conflict has not reached the standard. The best answers make a decision, defend it with evidence, and tie it to safety, risk, compliance and business outcome.

## What Every Strong Answer Contains

1. **Correct knowledge** — the right duty, regime, definition or concept.
2. **Application** — applied to the actual situation, not recited in the abstract.
3. **Decision** — a clear course of action, not "it depends".
4. **Justification** — why, in terms of risk, evidence and consequence.
5. **Safety priority** — life safety and statutory duties are never traded for convenience or cost.
6. **Business framing** — impact on continuity, reputation, revenue and visitor experience where relevant.
7. **Evidence and records** — the answer recognises that the record is the defence.

## Scoring Bands

| Band | Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| Distinction | 80-100% | Confident and decisive. Knowledge is correct and applied. Decisions are defended in risk and business language. Safety is instinctive. Evidence and follow-up are built in. |
| Pass | 60-79% | Sound. Knowledge mostly correct and applied. Decisions present but justification or evidence is thinner. Safety prioritised. |
| Borderline | 50-59% | Knowledge present but weakly applied. Decisions vague or hedged. Re-read the module and re-attempt. |
| Not yet | Below 50% | Recall only, misapplied or missing. No clear decision. Safety or compliance not prioritised. |

## Per-Question Marking Method

Award marks across four equal strands (each worth 25% of the question):

- **Knowledge (25%)** — is the underlying duty/concept correct?
- **Application (25%)** — is it applied to this situation?
- **Judgement (25%)** — is there a clear, defensible decision?
- **Communication (25%)** — is it clear, structured and pitched at the right audience?

A blank or wholly incorrect strand scores zero; partial credit is allowed within each strand.

## Automatic Caps

Cap an answer at **Borderline** regardless of other strengths if it:

- trades safety or a statutory duty for cost, speed or convenience;
- relies on contractor or staff reputation instead of evidence;
- accepts a serious risk without making the acceptance informed and documented;
- bluffs a figure or fact instead of committing to verify it.

These are the failure modes the course exists to remove. A polished answer that does one of them has missed the point.

## Red Flags (mark down)

- "Tell people to be more careful" as a primary control.
- Treating a near-miss as a non-event because no one was hurt.
- Wedged fire doors, blocked escape routes or disabled detection tolerated.
- Run-to-failure on safety-critical or revenue-critical assets with no contingency.
- Cutting compliance lines to hit a budget target.
- No records, no review date, no follow-up.

## Green Flags (mark up)

- Makes the area safe first, then solves the underlying problem.
- Prioritises by risk and consequence, not age or noise.
- Uses whole-life cost rather than lowest price.
- Flags bad news early with a recovery plan (no surprises).
- Translates the same facts for board, peers and workshop.
- Builds the record as the defence and sets a review date.

## Notes for an AI Tutor

- Mark against the model answer points for that question, but credit equally valid alternatives the learner justifies well.
- Always state the band, a one-line reason, the strongest part of the answer and the single most useful improvement.
- Identify the weak tag (for example `fire-safety`, `budgeting`) so progress tracking can route revision.
- Never invent a regulation; if unsure, mark the judgement and application, not contested legal detail.
