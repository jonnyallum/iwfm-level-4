## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this simulation, you should be able to:

- respond to multiple FM incidents under public, operational and board pressure;
- prioritise life safety, visitor welfare, animal welfare, compliance and continuity;
- brief senior leaders clearly without hiding uncertainty;
- create a credible 90-day stabilisation plan;
- create a 1-year improvement plan for estate resilience.

## Phone Lesson 1: Boardroom Thinking

In a senior FM role, the question is not only "what is broken?"

The board needs to know:

- what is the risk;
- who is affected;
- what is being done now;
- what decisions are needed;
- what evidence supports the position;
- when control will be restored.

Good board communication is calm, specific and honest. It avoids both panic and false reassurance.

## Phone Lesson 2: Visitor Attraction Pressure

A visitor attraction is a live public environment. Problems can affect guests, staff, contractors, animals, media reputation, ticket income, emergency services and regulators at the same time.

FM decisions must consider:

- public safety;
- crowd movement;
- animal welfare;
- accessibility;
- critical building systems;
- emergency planning;
- communications;
- evidence and legal duties.

## Phone Lesson 3: Prioritising Simultaneous Incidents

When several issues happen together, prioritise by consequence and control.

A practical order is:

1. Life safety and immediate danger.
2. Emergency response and communication.
3. Critical infrastructure and animal welfare.
4. Legal compliance and evidence.
5. Business continuity.
6. Recovery and prevention.

This does not mean lower items are ignored. It means the board can see why resources are being directed first to the highest consequences.

## Practical FM Application

You are the FM lead in a boardroom simulation. The estate is open, visitor numbers are high and several controls fail at once. Your role is to coordinate operations, contractors, evidence, communications and senior decisions.

You must avoid vague updates such as "we are looking into it". A better update is:

> We have isolated the affected area, confirmed no injuries, moved visitors to alternative routes, instructed the competent contractor, opened an incident log and will give the next status update at 14:30.

### Question 3

### Hard Question 2

## Simulation Incidents

### Power Outage With 5,000 Visitors Onsite

Immediate priorities:

- confirm life-safety systems status;
- check emergency lighting, fire alarm, communications and critical animal systems;
- activate incident command;
- coordinate visitor movement and accessible support;
- check generator or UPS status where present;
- communicate with operations, security and senior leadership;
- record timings and decisions.

Board concern:

- visitor safety;
- crowd control;
- ticketing and revenue;
- animal welfare;
- media and reputational impact.

### Fire Alarm Panel Fault

Immediate priorities:

- identify whether the system is impaired or still operational;
- call the fire alarm contractor;
- notify the Responsible Person or duty manager;
- consider fire watch or temporary measures;
- record panel messages and actions;
- escalate if evacuation detection or warning is compromised.

Board concern:

- whether the site can remain open safely;
- what compensatory measures are active;
- when full protection will be restored.

### Contractor Discovers Suspected Asbestos

Immediate priorities:

- stop work immediately;
- isolate the area;
- prevent disturbance and access;
- check asbestos register and survey scope;
- obtain competent asbestos advice;
- record who may have been exposed and what work occurred;
- communicate without speculation.

Board concern:

- health exposure;
- legal duties;
- contractor control failure;
- operational impact.

### Water Hygiene Records Missing

Immediate priorities:

- identify which records are missing and for what period;
- confirm current water system controls;
- speak to the Responsible Person and water hygiene contractor;
- prioritise high-risk outlets and vulnerable areas;
- complete urgent checks where needed;
- create a remedial evidence plan.

Board concern:

- whether control was lost;
- whether users are at risk;
- whether contractor or internal governance failed.

### Animal Enclosure Infrastructure Defect

Immediate priorities:

- protect staff, public and animals;
- notify animal management immediately;
- restrict access and create exclusion zones;
- assess whether containment, gates, barriers or services are compromised;
- involve competent engineering or specialist contractor support;
- record temporary controls and repair plan.

Board concern:

- animal welfare;
- public safety;
- operational continuity;
- regulatory and reputational risk.

### Maintenance Backlog With Reduced Budget

Immediate priorities:

- separate legal/life-safety backlog from discretionary works;
- risk-rate each item;
- identify assets with repeated failures;
- create options: fund now, phase, monitor or formally accept risk;
- show consequences of deferral;
- protect statutory compliance funding.

Board concern:

- affordability;
- risk appetite;
- assurance that cuts will not create unmanaged legal or safety exposure.

## 90-Day Plan

### Days 1-14: Stabilise

- Create a single incident and compliance action tracker.
- Confirm all life-safety systems status.
- Review fire alarm, emergency lighting, water hygiene, asbestos and critical electrical evidence.
- Identify immediate high-risk defects and temporary controls.
- Confirm emergency contacts and contractor call-out arrangements.
- Brief board weekly on red and amber risks.

### Days 15-45: Rebuild Control

- Audit statutory compliance evidence against the Compliance Bible matrix.
- Reconcile asset registers for critical systems and animal enclosure infrastructure.
- Review contractor approval, RAMS and permit-to-work controls.
- Close or escalate overdue high-risk actions.
- Agree budget decisions for life-safety and legal compliance items.
- Build dashboard reporting for FM Control Hub.

### Days 46-90: Prove Improvement

- Complete a board-level risk and compliance review.
- Publish a 12-month statutory compliance calendar.
- Define critical asset lifecycle priorities.
- Run a tabletop exercise for power outage and fire system impairment.
- Confirm water hygiene governance and Responsible Person arrangements.
- Report progress, remaining risk and funding decisions.

## 1-Year Plan

- Implement a complete asset and statutory compliance register.
- Integrate compliance evidence into FM Control Hub and Compliance Hub.
- Introduce risk-based lifecycle planning for critical assets.
- Formalise contractor management and permit-to-work governance.
- Develop visitor attraction emergency playbooks.
- Create animal infrastructure inspection and escalation routines.
- Build monthly board compliance reporting.
- Run two multi-team emergency exercises.
- Reduce maintenance backlog using criticality and risk scoring.
- Review lessons learned after peak season.

## Board-Level Talking Points

Use these phrases:

> Our immediate focus is life safety, visitor control, animal welfare and restoring critical systems.

> I am separating completed inspections from unresolved defects so the board has real assurance, not just paperwork completion.

> Where budget limits prevent immediate closure of lower-risk backlog, I will show the residual risk, temporary controls and decision required.

> We can remain operational only where compensatory measures keep risk tolerable and documented.

> The 90-day plan restores control; the 1-year plan improves resilience.

## Model Answers

### Model Answer 1

I would brief what is known, what is unknown, what controls are in place, who owns the next action and when the next update will be given. I would avoid guessing technical causes before competent advice is available.

### Model Answer 2

From operations I need visitor numbers, crowd issues and closure options. From animal teams I need welfare-critical systems and enclosure risks. From contractors I need technical status and timescales. From visitor services I need guest communications, accessibility concerns and complaints or incidents.

### Model Answer 3

The decision depends on whether life-safety systems, crowd control, animal welfare and critical infrastructure can be kept within tolerable risk. If controls are impaired and compensatory measures are not enough, partial or full closure is the correct decision.

### Model Answer 4

I would create an incident command structure with a single lead, workstream owners for life safety, operations, animal infrastructure, contractors, communications and evidence. I would prioritise immediate danger first, then critical systems, then compliance evidence and recovery.

### Model Answer 5

I would tell the board that weaknesses have been identified, temporary controls are in place, owners and dates are assigned and the highest-risk gaps are being addressed first. I would be clear where decisions on funding or closure are needed.

## Marking Guide

Strong answers include:

- calm incident command;
- life-safety and animal welfare prioritisation;
- clear communication under uncertainty;
- evidence discipline;
- contractor and competent-person escalation;
- board-level risk framing;
- practical 90-day and 1-year planning.

Weak answers overpromise, hide uncertainty, ignore visitors or animals, or treat missing records as a minor admin problem.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> In a live visitor attraction, I would prioritise life safety, visitor control, animal welfare and critical systems first, while keeping a clear incident log and giving the board honest options with risks, controls and decisions needed.
